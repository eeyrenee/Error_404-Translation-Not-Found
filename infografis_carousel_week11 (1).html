Lexical Error

"Boring" vs "Bored"
— They're not the same

Incorrect
I am boring. → Saya membosankan.
Correct
I am bored. → Saya bosan.
Indonesian uses one word "bosan" for both states. English splits them: boring = causes boredom; bored = experiencing it. Classic L1 transfer.
3 / 8
Lexical Error

"She has 20 years"
— Age needs "to be"

Incorrect
She has 20 years. → Dia punya 20 tahun.
Correct
She is 20 years old.
Indonesian uses "punya" (have) for age. English uses to be. This structural mismatch is predicted directly by Contrastive Analysis (Lado, 1957).
4 / 8
Syntactic Error

The missing "to be"
& auxiliary verbs

Incorrect
He very smart. / Why you not come?
Correct
He is very smart. / Why didn't you come?
Indonesian has no copula — "Dia sangat pintar" is complete. Learners transfer this zero-copula pattern into English. Questions also need auxiliary inversion (interlanguage error).
5 / 8
Semantic Error

Idioms can't be
decoded word-by-word

Incorrect
"Open the lamp." / "Dia melewati jauh."
Correct
"Turn on the lamp." / "He passed away."
"Open" ≠ "turn on" for devices. "Passed away" = died — not a literal movement. Idioms must be learned as lexical chunks, not decoded component by component.
6 / 8
Pragmatic Error

Fixed expressions
break when modified

Incorrect
"Rest in peace for you." / "He is my senior."
Correct
"Rest in peace." / "He is my senior colleague."
"Rest in peace" is a formulaic expression — adding "for you" breaks it socially. "Senior" in English always needs context (colleague, student). Pragmatic transfer from Indonesian.
7 / 8
SLA Theory

Why do these errors happen?

L1
Language Transfer (Lado, 1957)
Indonesian patterns bleed into English output — especially copula, age structure, and intensifiers.
IL
Interlanguage (Selinker, 1972)
Learners build an internal grammar that is "almost English" — hypotheses that are close but not yet target-like.
LT
Literal Translation
Word-by-word processing ignores idiom, context, and social convention — producing correct grammar but wrong meaning.
3 Golden Rules

How to avoid
these errors

1
Think meaning, not words. Ask what the sentence is trying to DO, not just say.
2
Learn idioms as whole chunks. Fixed expressions can't be decoded — memorize them complete.
3
Notice where Indonesian diverges. Copula, auxiliaries, and intensifiers are high-risk zones. Give them extra attention.
